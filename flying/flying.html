About me flying

I always dreamed of flying, the ultimate expression of freedom by nevigating in three dimension and countering Earth's gravity. As an astronoer, flying bring me a few hundred meters closer to the objects I study, an ignorable distance in astronomy but a significient distance for a human being. Flying offers an opprtunity to see the sky even in cloudy days by flying over the clouds (although I learned doing so is not recommanded for a private pilot flying Visual Flight Rule (VFR) because going through the cloud is not allowed by regulations, and flying above the cloud is allowed but not recommanded because it can be hard and dangerous come back down (through the cloud). I guess this will be the motivation for certifying for Instrument Rating one day).

The motivation drove me to acquire a private pilot certificate during my Ph.D. time at the University of Virginia. Unlike driving where the option of "stopping" always exists, flying requires constant decision making and judgement, even when something unexpected happens, for example, an engine failure (which pilots are trained to handel), or an unforseen but close passage with another aircraft that could have ended in a mid-air collision (ask me, if you are interested)..
